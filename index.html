    What does this tool do?

    Photo Engraving Online converts a regular photo into a rasterized black-and-white image optimized for CO2 or diode laser engraving. You can then import the resulting PNG directly into your laser software (LightBurn, LaserGRBL, XCS, etc.) and engrave it.

    Step-by-step guide
    1

    Upload a photo

    Drag the file into the upload area or click to choose it from your device. Supported formats are JPG, PNG, BMP and WebP. For best results, use a sharp photo with good contrast.

    2

    Set DPI

    Choose a resolution that matches your material and your laser’s capabilities. See the recommendation table below.

    3

    Set the output size in mm

    Enter the final engraving width in millimeters. The tool automatically calculates the height, keeps the aspect ratio and shows the resulting pixel count.

    4

    Fine-tune the width

    The slider lets you fine-tune the output width in pixels. It stays synced with the width field in mm, so changing one automatically updates the other.

    5

    Check the output and download the PNG

    The output shows the final rasterized image. If it looks good, click Download PNG and import the file into your laser software.

    Recommended DPI by material
    DPI Best for Note
    96 DPI Fabric, leather, rough wood, quick previews Fastest setting, low detail; good for large engravings and testing.
    150 DPI Soft wood, plywood, cardboard, large photos Draft-quality detail with less risk of overburn on absorbent materials.
    254 DPI ✓ Wood photos, MDF, slate, stone, glass LightBurn-style 0.10 mm spacing; default starting point with a strong quality-to-speed balance.
    300 DPI Most photo engravings: wood, glass, slate, MDF, plastic LightBurn-style 0.10 mm spacing; default starting point with a strong quality-to-speed balance.
    508 DPI Acrylic, laminates, coated metals, anodized aluminum, stainless steel, ceramics Highest detail in this tool; best for fiber laser metal marking or coated/marked stainless steel. Slower engraving, always test first.
    How to choose the correct output size?

    The output size in mm should exactly match the real physical size of the engraving area on your material.

    Example: If you want to engrave a portrait on a wooden plate with a width of 80 mm, set the width to 80 mm and choose 254 DPI. The tool will create an image about 800 px wide, which matches the required physical size.

    ℹ️ Formula: width in px = (width in mm ÷ 25.4) × DPI

    Tips for better results
    📷 Input photo quality
    Use sharp photos with good contrast. Dark or blurry images will produce worse engraving results even at high DPI.
    🔲 Contrast and brightness
    Before uploading, try adjusting the photo in any editor. Higher contrast often improves the final rasterized result a lot.
    🧪 Test engraving
    Always test the result first on an offcut of the same material before running the final engraving.
    ⚙️ LightBurn settings
    Import the PNG as Passthrough or Threshold. The dithering is already included in the file, so additional image processing may make it worse.
    Photo engraving FAQ
    Is this photo to laser engraving converter free?
    Yes. The tool is free to use in your browser. You can convert a photo to a laser engraving PNG and download the result without installing software.
    Which laser software can use the exported PNG?
    The PNG output is suitable for LightBurn, XCS, LaserGRBL, EZCAD and other laser engraving software that imports raster images.
    Does the PNG include DPI information?
    Yes. The exported PNG includes DPI metadata, which helps laser software keep the correct physical engraving size.
    Can I use my own DPI value?
    Yes. You can choose a preset DPI or enter a custom DPI value for your laser, material and preferred line spacing.
    What does Invert Colors do?
    Invert Colors creates a negative output by swapping black and white in the final laser-ready image.

    No. Photos are processed locally in your browser and are not uploaded to our server.
