QR code best practices

How to create a QR code that scans reliably

Keep it static when the destination will not change

Static QR codes store the actual content inside the QR pattern. They are ideal for WiFi credentials, contact cards, permanent pages, product labels and offline instructions.

Use strong contrast

Dark modules on a light background are the safest choice. Avoid low-contrast colors, busy photos behind the QR code and transparent backgrounds on dark materials.

Choose the right error correction

Use M for clean black-and-white codes, Q for tougher print conditions and H when adding a logo or when the code may be scratched, dusty or partly covered.

Respect the quiet zone

Leave empty space around the QR code. A 4-module margin helps scanners identify the code, especially on stickers, flyers, menus and packaging.

Do not overfill the code

Long vCards, event descriptions and payment notes create denser QR codes. Keep the payload short whenever possible for faster scanning.

Test before printing

Scan the final PNG on multiple phones, from the real viewing distance, and after placing it into the final design or print artwork.

Static QR codes explained

How a static QR code works

Encoded directly into the image

A static QR code stores the final text, URL, WiFi login, vCard, event or payment data inside the QR pattern. Once the QR image is downloaded or printed, the encoded content stays the same.

  • No account needed
  • No QR database
  • No saved payloads
  • Fast browser-based generation
  • Works as long as the encoded content remains valid

Best for print and simple sharing

Static QR codes are ideal for labels, product packaging, business cards, menus, flyers, posters, WiFi signs and payment instructions. Check the destination carefully before printing, because the QR image contains the final data.

  • Download as high-resolution PNG
  • Use strong contrast for scanning
  • Keep enough quiet zone around the code
  • Test on multiple phones before printing
  • Keep URLs and text as short as practical
